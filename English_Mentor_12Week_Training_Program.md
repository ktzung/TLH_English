## 🗓️ TUẦN 1: Breaking the Ice — Bắt đầu giao tiếp

### Academic Speaking Patterns

```
Pattern 1: Self-introduction
"I'm [name], a [position] at [institution]. My research focuses on [topic]."

Pattern 2: Stating your role
"I work as a [school counselor / lecturer / researcher] specializing in [area]."

Pattern 3: Expressing interest
"I'm particularly interested in how [topic] affects [outcome]."
```

### Counseling Communication Patterns

```
Pattern 1: Welcoming
"Welcome. I'm glad you're here today. How are you feeling?"

"It's completely normal to feel that way. Many students experience this."

```

```

## 🗓️ TUẦN 4: Academic Discussion Skills — Thảo luận học thuật

### Mục tiêu tuần
- Tham gia thảo luận học thuật tự tin
- Đồng ý/không đồng ý một cách lịch sự
- Đặt câu hỏi phản biện

### Kỹ năng trọng tâm
- Agreeing and disagreeing politely
- Asking critical questions
- Building on others' ideas

### Chủ đề học thuật
- "Debates in educational research"
- "Ethical considerations in education studies"
- "The role of evidence in educational policy"

### Vocabulary chuyên ngành (35 từ mới)

| Từ vựng | Nghĩa | Ví dụ |
|---------|-------|-------|
| I respectfully disagree | tôi không đồng ý một cách tôn trọng | "I respectfully disagree with that point." |
| That's a valid point | đó là quan điểm hợp lý | "That's a valid point, however..." |
| I'd like to build on that | tôi muốn bổ sung thêm | "I'd like to build on that idea." |
| counterargument | phản luận | "One counterargument could be..." |
| consensus | đồng thuận | "We seem to have reached a consensus." |
| empirical evidence | bằng chứng thực nghiệm | "The empirical evidence suggests..." |
| generalizability | tính tổng quát hóa | "The generalizability is limited." |
| ethical clearance | phê duyệt đạo đức | "We obtained ethical clearance from..." |
| informed consent | đồng thuận có hiểu biết | "All participants gave informed consent." |
| bias | thiên kiến | "We need to address potential bias." |

### Academic Speaking Patterns

```
Polite disagreement:
"I see your point, but I wonder if [alternative view] might also apply here."

Building on ideas:
"That's an excellent observation. It reminds me of [related concept]."

Asking critical questions:
"Could you elaborate on how you controlled for [variable]?"

Requesting clarification:
"I'm not sure I follow. Could you rephrase that?"
```

### Counseling Communication Patterns

```
Validating emotions in discussion:
"I can see this topic is really important to you."

Managing disagreement empathetically:
"I understand your perspective. Let me share a different viewpoint."
```

### Speaking Drills

**Drill 1 — Agree/Disagree Sprint (15 phút)**
> Đọc 5 tuyên bố về giáo dục. Với mỗi tuyên bố, nói 1 câu đồng ý + 1 câu không đồng ý.

**Drill 2 — Question Builder (10 phút)**
> Nghe 1 đoạn thuyết trình. Đặt 3 câu hỏi phản biện.

**Drill 3 — Discussion Chain (15 phút)**
> Thảo luận 1 chủ đề với AI partner. Mỗi lượt phải build on ý trước.

 to my own work on [topic]. Could you tell me more about [detail]?"
```

### Maintaining Conversation

```
Showing interest:
"That's fascinating. How did you come to study that?"
"What has been your most surprising finding?"
"How do you see this developing in the next few years?"

Sharing your perspective:
"That resonates with my experience. In my context, [observation]."
"I've found something similar in my research on [topic]."
"That's an interesting approach. I've been using [method] instead."

Finding common ground:
"It sounds like we share an interest in [topic]."
"Have you read [relevant paper]? It connects to what you're describing."
"We should definitely keep in touch about this."
```

### Graceful Exit Strategies

```
"I've really enjoyed our conversation. Let me give you my card. 
 I'll follow up with an email about [specific topic]."

"It's been wonderful meeting you. I don't want to take up too much 
 of your time, but I'd love to continue this conversation. 
 Could I get your email?"

"Thank you so much for sharing your insights. 
 I'll definitely look into [suggestion they made]. 
 Enjoy the rest of the conference!"
```

## Module 5.3: Research Presentation Structure

### 10-Minute Conference Presentation Template

```
[SLIDE 1] Title & Introduction (1 phút)
"Good morning/afternoon, everyone. Thank you for being here. 
 Today I'll be presenting our research on [TITLE]. 
 My co-authors are [NAMES] from [INSTITUTIONS]."

[SLIDE 2] Background & Problem (2 phút)
"Let me start with some background. 
 [Context about the topic]. 
 However, there's a gap in our understanding of [gap]. 
 This is important because [significance]."

[SLIDE 3] Research Questions (30 giây)
"Our study addresses two main questions: 
 First, [RQ1]. Second, [RQ2]."

[SLIDE 4] Methodology (2 phút)
"We employed a [design] approach. 
 Participants were [description], with a total of [N]. 
 Data was collected using [instruments]. 
 We analyzed the data using [analysis method]."

[SLIDE 5-6] Findings (3 phút)
"Let me walk you through our key findings. 
 As you can see from [figure/table], [finding 1]. 
 Additionally, [finding 2]. 
 Interestingly, [finding 3]."

[SLIDE 7] Discussion & Implications (1 phút)
"These findings suggest that [interpretation]. 
 The implications for practice include [implications]. 
 However, I want to acknowledge [limitation]."

[SLIDE 8] Conclusion & Future Research (30 giây)
"To sum up, [main takeaway]. 
 Future research should [suggestions]. 
 Thank you for your attention. I'll now open the floor for questions."
```

## Module 5.4: Handling Q&A

### Common Questions & Response Strategies

```
Question about methodology:
"Thank you for that question. We chose [method] because [reason]. 
 One alternative would have been [alternative], but we opted for [choice] due to [reason]."

Question about limitations:
"That's a great point. You're right that [limitation] is a concern. 
 We tried to mitigate this by [strategy]. 
 Future research with [improvement] would strengthen the findings."

Question you don't know the answer to:
"That's an excellent question, and honestly, I don't have a definitive answer. 
 It's something we've been thinking about. My preliminary view is [tentative answer], 
 but I'd be very interested in your thoughts on this."

Challenging question:
"I appreciate the critical perspective. 
 That's a valid concern. Let me think about that... 
 I believe [response]. However, I acknowledge that [concession]."
```

## Module 6.2: Explaining Educational Concepts

### Framework for Clear Explanations

```
Step 1: State the concept
"Today we're going to learn about [CONCEPT]."

Step 2: Define it
"[CONCEPT] is defined as [DEFINITION]."

Step 3: Give an example
"For example, [REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE]."

Step 4: Use analogy
"Think of it like [ANALOGY]."

Step 5: Check understanding
"Can anyone give me another example?"
```

### Example: Explaining "Scaffolding"

```
"Today we're going to learn about scaffolding in education.

Scaffolding is a teaching method where teachers provide temporary support 
to help students achieve a learning goal they couldn't reach on their own.

For example, when teaching essay writing, a teacher might first provide 
a template, then an outline, then gradually remove support until 
the student can write independently.

Think of it like training wheels on a bicycle. 
You need them at first, but eventually, you ride on your own.

Can anyone think of a time when you used scaffolding in your teaching?"
```

## Module 7.3: Counseling Conversation Frameworks

### Initial Session Framework

```
1. WELCOME (1 phút)
"Welcome. I'm glad you're here. 
 This is a confidential space where you can share freely. 
 How are you feeling today?"

2. EXPLORATION (3 phút)
"Can you tell me what brought you here today?"
"Tell me more about that."
"How long has this been going on?"
"How is this affecting your daily life?"

3. UNDERSTANDING (2 phút)
"So it sounds like [summarize]. Is that right?"
"How does that make you feel?"
"What have you tried so far?"

4. STRATEGIZING (3 phút)
"Let's think about what might help."
"What has worked for you in the past?"
"I'd like to suggest [strategy]. What do you think?"

5. CLOSING (1 phút)
"Thank you for sharing with me today. 
 I want you to know that I'm here for you. 
 Let's schedule another session for [date]. 
 In the meantime, remember [key takeaway]."
```

### Crisis Response Framework

```
1. STAY CALM
"I'm here. You're safe."
"Take a deep breath with me."

2. ASSESS
"Can you tell me what's happening right now?"
"Are you safe at this moment?"

3. LISTEN
"I hear you. This is really difficult."
"You don't have to go through this alone."

4. PLAN
"Let's create a plan to keep you safe."
"Who can we reach out to for support?"

5. ACT
"I'm going to [action] to make sure you're okay."
"Let's contact [resource] together."
```

## Module 7.4: Discussing Sensitive Topics

### Mental Health Vocabulary

```
Instead of: "You're depressed"
Say: "It sounds like you've been feeling persistently sad"

Instead of: "You have a problem"
Say: "You're going through a challenging time"

Instead of: "You need to fix this"
Say: "Let's work on strategies to help you feel better"

Instead of: "That's not a big deal"
Say: "I can see this is really important to you"

Instead of: "Just try harder"
Say: "Let's explore what might help you move forward"
```

### Cultural Sensitivity Phrases

```
"I want to be respectful of your cultural background."
"How does your culture view this situation?"
"I'd like to understand your perspective better."
"Everyone's experience is unique. Can you help me understand yours?"
```
