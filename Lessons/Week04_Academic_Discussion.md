### 🗣️ B. SPEAKING PATTERNS

#### Pattern 1: Polite Disagreement

```
LEVEL 1 — Mild (Nhẹ nhàng):
"I see your point, but I wonder if [alternative] might also apply."
"That's interesting. However, have you considered [alternative]?"
"I appreciate that perspective. Another way to look at it is..."

LEVEL 2 — Moderate (Trung bình):
"I respectfully disagree because [reason]."
"While I understand your position, I believe [alternative]."
"I'd like to offer a different perspective based on [evidence]."

LEVEL 3 — Strong (Mạnh mẽ, nhưng lịch sự):
"With all due respect, I have to disagree because [strong reason]."
"I'd challenge that assumption. The evidence suggests [alternative]."
"I see the logic, but I think there's a fundamental issue with [concern]."
```

#### Pattern 2: Building on Ideas

```
"Yes, and furthermore..."
"Building on what [name] said, I'd add..."
"That's an excellent point. It reminds me of [related concept]."
"Extending that idea, what if we also consider..."
"I agree with [point], and I think it connects to [broader theme]."
```

#### Pattern 3: Asking Critical Questions

```
SUPPORTIVE QUESTIONS (ủng hộ):
"Could you tell us more about [aspect]?"
"How did you arrive at that conclusion?"

CHALLENGING QUESTIONS (thách thức):
"What evidence supports that claim?"
"Have you considered alternative explanations?"
"How would this apply in a different context?"
"What are the limitations of this approach?"

CLARIFYING QUESTIONS (làm rõ):
"I'm not sure I follow. Could you rephrase that?"
"When you say [term], do you mean [interpretation]?"
"Could you give a specific example of...?"
```

#### Pattern 4: Managing Discussion Flow

```
Starting a discussion:
"I'd like to open the discussion by posing this question: [question]."
"Let's begin by examining [topic]. What are your thoughts?"

Transitioning:
"Let's move on to the next point."
"Building on that, I'd like to raise another issue."
"That leads us to the question of..."

Summarizing:
"Let me summarize what we've discussed so far."
"To pull together the main points..."
"It sounds like we agree on [point] but differ on [point]."
```

### 🎧 C. LISTENING PRACTICE

#### Activity 1: Academic Panel Discussion

**Nguồn:** Tìm "education panel discussion" hoặc "academic debate education" trên YouTube (20 phút)

```
Pre-listening:
1. Xem tiêu đề panel → Dự đoán chủ đề
2. Liệt kê 3 quan điểm có thể được thảo luận

While-listening:
Lần 1: Ghi lại cách người ta ĐỒNG Ý
→ "I agree because..."
→ "That's a great point..."
Lần 2: Ghi lại cách người ta KHÔNG ĐỒNG Ý
→ "I see it differently..."
→ "However, I think..."
Lần 3: Ghi lại câu hỏi phản biện
→ "What about...?"
→ "How do you explain...?"

Post-listening:
1. Chọn 1 câu hay nhất từ mỗi loại
2. Shadow 3 câu đó
3. Viết 3 câu tương tự cho chủ đề của bạn
```

### 🎤 D. SHADOWING EXERCISE

**Đoạn 1: Agreeing & Adding (30 giây)**
> "I completely agree with the previous speaker's point about the importance of evidence-based practice. In fact, I'd like to build on that by sharing an example from my own research. When we implemented a data-driven approach in Vietnamese schools, we saw a remarkable improvement in student outcomes."

**Đoạn 2: Respectful Disagreement (35 giây)**
> "I appreciate that perspective, and I can see the logic behind it. However, I'd like to offer a different viewpoint based on my experience working in Southeast Asian contexts. The assumption that standardized testing is universally effective doesn't account for cultural differences in how students learn and demonstrate knowledge."

**Đoạn 3: Asking Critical Questions (30 giây)**
> "That raises an interesting question. How do we ensure that our interventions are culturally appropriate? Have you considered how this approach might work differently in collectivist versus individualist societies? I think this is an important dimension that deserves more attention."

### 🔤 E. PRONUNCIATION FOCUS

#### Discussion phrases — Trọng âm và ngữ điệu

```
CÂU ĐỒNG Ý:
"I RE-spect-fully a-GREE." → Giọng đi lên nhẹ ở cuối
"That's a VA-lid POINT." → Nhấn "valid" và "point"
"I'd like to BUILD on THAT." → Nhấn "build" và "that"

CÂU KHÔNG ĐỒNG Ý:
"With all due re-SPECT..." → Nhấn "respect"
"I'd CHAL-lenge that as-SUMP-tion." → Nhấn "challenge" và "assumption"
"I see your POINT, but..." → Nhấn "point", giọng xuống nhẹ ở "but"

CÂU HỎI:
"Could you e-LAB-o-rate?" → Giọng LÊN cuối câu
"What EVIDENCE supports that?" → Nhấn "evidence"
"How MIGHT this ap-PLY?" → Giọng LÊN cuối câu
```

### 🎭 F. ROLE-PLAY

#### Scenario 1: Panel Discussion — "Should AI Be Used in School Counseling?"

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
📍 ĐỊA ĐIỂM: Academic seminar panel
👥 NHÂN VẬT: Moderator + Panelist A + Bạn (Panelist B)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Moderator: "Let's discuss: Should AI tools be used in school counseling? 
            Panelist A, what are your thoughts?"

Panelist A: "I believe AI has great potential. Chatbots can provide 
             24/7 support, handle initial screenings, and reach students 
             who might be too shy to talk to a counselor."

Moderator: "Panelist B, do you agree?"

You: "That's a thought-provoking question. I appreciate Panelist A's 
      point about accessibility — AI can indeed reach more students. 
      However, I'd like to offer a different perspective.
      
      From my experience as a school counselor, the therapeutic 
      relationship — the human connection — is at the heart of 
      effective counseling. AI can support the process, but it 
      should never replace the human element.
      
      I'd also raise an ethical concern: How do we ensure 
      confidentiality when AI handles sensitive student data? 
      What about students in crisis who need immediate human intervention?
      
      I believe the ideal approach is AI as a supplementary tool — 
      perhaps for scheduling, psychoeducation, or initial screening — 
      while keeping human counselors at the center of the process."

Panelist A: "That's a valid point. But what about schools that can't 
             afford enough counselors?"

You: "I take your point about resource constraints. That's a real 
      challenge, especially in developing countries. Perhaps the solution 
      is to use AI strategically — not as a replacement, but as a way 
      to free up counselor time for the cases that need human attention. 
      Could you elaborate on how you envision AI handling more complex cases?"

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

```
              in education because it captures the richness 
              of student experiences."

You: "I see your point about the depth of qualitative data. 
      However, I'd challenge the idea that it's inherently 
      more valuable. Both approaches have their strengths.
      
      Quantitative research allows us to identify patterns 
      across large populations and establish statistical

      and context. That's why I prefer mixed methods — 
      it combines the best of both worlds."

Colleague B: "But mixed methods requires more time and resources."

You: "That's a valid concern. It does require more investment. 
      However, the richer data often justifies the additional effort. 
      What do others think about this trade-off?"
```

```
TOPIC: Choose one education debate and write your opinion

STRUCTURE:
1. State the issue (2 câu)
2. Present opposing views (4 câu)
```

### 💡 TIPS cho Tuần 4

```
🌟 Mẹo 1: LUÔN ĐỒNG Ý TRƯỚC, RỒI MỚI PHẢN ĐỐI
   → "I see your point, BUT..."
   → "That's valid, HOWEVER..."
   → "I appreciate that, YET..."
   → Nghe lịch sự hơn "I disagree because..."

🌟 Mẹo 2: DÙNG "I" STATEMENTS
   → "I believe..." (không phải "You're wrong")
   → "In my experience..." (không phải "The facts show")
   → "I'd suggest..." (không phải "We should")
   → Cá nhân hóa quan điểm = lịch sự hơn

🌟 Mẹo 3: CHUẨN BỊ CÂU HỎI SẴN
   → Trước mỗi discussion, viết 3 câu hỏi
   → Khi nghe, tìm cơ hội sử dụng
   → Không biết hỏi gì? Hỏi: "Could you elaborate?"

🌟 Mẹo 4: PARAPHRASE TRƯỚC KHI PHẢN ĐỐI
   → "So what you're saying is [paraphrase]. 
      Is that correct? If so, I'd like to offer..."
   → Thể hiện bạn LẮNG NGHE trước khi phản biện

🌟 Mẹo 5: PRACTICE "BRIDGE" PHRASES
   → "That brings me to another point..."
   → "This connects to what [name] said earlier..."
   → "Building on that idea..."
   → Giữ discussion flow tự nhiên
```
