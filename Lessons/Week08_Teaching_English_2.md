### 🗣️ B. SPEAKING PATTERNS

#### Pattern 1: Giving Constructive Feedback

```
STRUCTURE (Sandwich method):

[STRENGTH] → "You've done a great job with [specific strength]."
[SUGGESTION] → "One suggestion for improvement would be [specific area]."
[ENCOURAGEMENT] → "Overall, this is [positive evaluation]. Keep it up!"

EXAMPLES:

"You've done a great job connecting theory to practice in your analysis. 
 One suggestion for improvement would be to strengthen your conclusion 
 by adding a specific recommendation. 
 Overall, this is solid work that demonstrates good understanding of the topic."

"I appreciate the depth of your research. 
 Your literature review is comprehensive. 
 However, your methodology section could be more detailed — 
 specifically, explain why you chose this particular method. 
 Overall, this shows great potential."
```

#### Pattern 2: Facilitating Discussion

```
OPENING:
"Today's discussion topic is [TOPIC]. 
 Let's start with a question: [QUESTION]?"

ENCOURAGING PARTICIPATION:
"What do others think about this point?"
"Does anyone have a different perspective?"
"Let's hear from someone who hasn't shared yet."

BUILDING ON RESPONSES:
"That's an excellent observation. Can you elaborate?"
"I'd like to build on what [NAME] said."
"How does this connect to what we learned about [TOPIC]?"

CHALLENGING THINKING:
"To play devil's advocate, what if [COUNTERPOINT]?"
"What's the underlying assumption here?"
"How would you respond to someone who disagrees?"

CLOSING:
"Let me summarize the key points from our discussion."
"To bring this together, we've identified [MAIN POINTS]."
"The take-home message from today is [MESSAGE]."
```

#### Pattern 3: Higher-Order Questions (Bloom's Taxonomy)

```
REMEMBER:
"What is the definition of...?"
"Can you list the stages of...?"

UNDERSTAND:
"Can you explain in your own words...?"
"What is the main idea of...?"

APPLY:
"How would you use [concept] in a real classroom?"
"What would happen if...?"

ANALYZE:
"What are the differences between...?"
"Why do you think [author] argued that?"

EVALUATE:
"Do you agree or disagree? Why?"
"What are the strengths and weaknesses of...?"

CREATE:
"How would you design a program that...?"
"What alternative solution would you propose?"
```

### 🎤 D. SHADOWING EXERCISE

**Đoạn 1: Giving Feedback (35 giây)**
> "Let me share some feedback on your assignment. First, I really appreciate the effort you put into your literature review — it's comprehensive and well-organized. One area I'd suggest developing further is your analysis section. Try to connect the evidence more explicitly to your research question. Overall, this is strong work that shows good understanding of the topic."

**Đoạn 2: Facilitating Discussion (35 giây)**
> "Today we're discussing the role of standardized testing in education. Let's start with a question: Do you think standardized tests accurately measure student learning? Take a moment to think, then share with your partner. After that, we'll discuss as a class. I want to hear different perspectives — there's no right or wrong answer here."

### 🔤 E. PRONUNCIATION FOCUS — Feedback & Discussion Voice

```
FEEDBACK — Ấm áp, khuyến khích:
"I ap-pre-ci-ate your ef-fort." → Nhấn "appreciate" và "effort"
"One AREA to im-PROVE." → Nhấn "area" và "improve"
"Can you e-LAB-o-rate?" → Giọng tò mò, không phê phán
"That's IN-sight-ful." → Giọng khen ngợi thực sự

DISCUSSION — Năng lượng, mở:
"What do OTH-ers think?" → Giọng mở, mời gọi
"Let's hear from SOME-one who hasn't SHARED." → Ấm áp
"How MIGHT this ap-PLY?" → Giọng tò mò
"Let's DIG DEEP-er." → Nhấn "deeper"
```

### 🎭 F. ROLE-PLAY

#### Scenario 1: Facilitating a Seminar on Social-Emotional Learning

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
📍 Graduate seminar — 8 students
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Teacher (bạn): "Good afternoon, everyone. Today we're discussing 
                 social-emotional learning, or SEL. 
                 Let's start with a question: 
                 Why do you think SEL has become such an important 
                 topic in education recently? 
                 
                 [Pause — wait 5 seconds]
                 
                 Sarah, would you like to start?"

Student A: "I think it's because we're seeing more mental health 
            issues among students."

Teacher: "That's a great observation, Sarah. Can you elaborate 
          on what you mean by 'more mental health issues'?"

Student A: "Well, anxiety and depression rates have increased, 
            especially after the pandemic."

Teacher: "Thank you. Does anyone have a different perspective 
          on why SEL has gained importance?"

Student B: "I think it's also about preparing students for the 
            workforce. Employers value emotional intelligence."

Teacher: "That's an insightful point. It connects education 
          to broader life skills. 
          
          Now, let's dig deeper. Some critics argue that 
          SEL takes time away from academics. 
          How would you respond to that?"

Student C: "I'd argue that SEL actually improves academic outcomes 
            because students learn better when they feel safe."

Teacher: "Excellent argument. Can you give a specific example?"

Student C: "A study showed that schools implementing SEL programs 
            saw a 10% increase in test scores."

Teacher: "Very well argued. Let me build on that — 
          research by Durlak et al. (2011) found that SEL programs 
          improve academic performance by 11 percentile points. 
          
          Let's wrap up. The take-home message is that 
          SEL is not separate from academics — it's foundational to it. 
          For next week, please read the CASEL framework 
          and come prepared to discuss implementation strategies."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

#### Scenario 2: Giving Feedback on a Student Paper

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
📍 Office hours — Student submits draft
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Teacher (bạn): "Thank you for submitting your draft. Let me walk you 
                 through my feedback. 
                 
                 First, I want to acknowledge your strengths. 
                 Your literature review is comprehensive — you've clearly 
                 done extensive reading. Your writing is clear and organized. 
                 
                 Now, one area I'd suggest developing is your methodology. 
                 Right now, it's quite brief. I'd like you to explain 
                 more about why you chose this particular method and 
                 how you ensured validity. 
                 
                 Also, your conclusion could be stronger. 
                 Instead of just summarizing, try to discuss the 
                 implications of your findings for practice. 
                 
                 Overall, this is solid work that shows good potential. 
                 I'm confident that with these revisions, it will be 
                 a strong paper. Do you have any questions?"

Student: "Should I add more references to the methodology section?"

Teacher: "Yes, definitely. Citing methodological literature that supports 
          your approach would strengthen that section considerably. 
          For example, you could reference Creswell's work on 
          mixed methods design."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 💡 TIPS cho Tuần 8

```
🌟 Mẹo 1: FEEDBACK = CỤ THỂ
   → ❌ "Good job" (chung chung, vô nghĩa)
   → ✅ "Your use of evidence in paragraph 3 is very effective"
   → Càng cụ thể → càng hữu ích

🌟 Mẹo 2: ĐỢI SAU CÂU HỎI
   → Hỏi xong → Im lặng 5-10 giây
   → Đếm trong đầu: "1 Mississippi, 2 Mississippi..."
   → Sinh viên cần thời gian → ĐỪNG vội trả lời

🌟 Mẹo 3: SỬ DỤNG "THINK-PAIR-SHARE"
   → Giảm áp lực nói trước lớp
   → Mỗi người đều được tham gia
   → Teacher chỉ facilitate, không dominate

🌟 Mẹo 4: CHALLENGE BẰNG CÂU HỎI
   → Thay vì "You're wrong" → "What if we consider...?"
   → Thay vì "I disagree" → "How would you respond to critics?"
   → Dùng câu hỏi để thúc đẩy tư duy

🌟 Mẹo 5: TỔNG KẾT CUỐI MỖI BUỔI
   → "Today we discussed 3 main points..."
   → "The key takeaway is..."
   → "For next time, please..."
   → Giúp sinh viên nhớ nội dung
```
