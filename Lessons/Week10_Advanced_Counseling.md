### 🗣️ B. SPEAKING PATTERNS

#### Pattern 1: Addressing Self-Harm

```
"I've noticed [signs]. I want you to know that I'm here for you."
"Your safety is my top priority."
"Can you tell me more about what's been happening?"
"Let's create a plan to keep you safe."
"I want to make sure you have support. Who can we reach out to?"
"I'm going to [action] to make sure you're okay."
```

#### Pattern 2: Creating a Safety Plan

```
"Let's create a plan for when you're feeling [emotion]."
"First, what are the warning signs that you're struggling?"
"Who can you reach out to when you need support?"
"What activities help you calm down?"
"Let's write down some emergency contacts."
"Remember, this plan is yours. You can use it whenever you need."
```

#### Pattern 3: Communicating with Parents

```
OPENING:
"Thank you for coming in today. I appreciate you taking the time."
"I want to emphasize that we're here to support [student]."
"Our goal is to work together for [student]'s success."

SHARING CONCERNS:
"I'd like to share some observations about [student]."
"At school, we've noticed [specific behaviors]."
"This is concerning because [impact]."

LISTENING TO PARENTS:
"I'd like to hear your perspective."
"What have you noticed at home?"
"How does [student] describe their experience?"

COLLABORATING:
"Here's what I'd suggest we try."
"Let's develop a plan together."
"Consistent support at home and school will make a difference."

CLOSING:
"Thank you for your partnership."
"Let's schedule a follow-up in [timeframe]."
"Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have concerns."
```

### 🎭 F. ROLE-PLAY

#### Scenario 1: Crisis Response — Self-Harm Disclosure

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
📍 Văn phòng tham vấn
👥 NHÂN VẬT: Student + Counselor (bạn)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Student: "I need to tell you something. I've been... 
          hurting myself."

Counselor: "Thank you for trusting me with this. 
            I'm here for you, and your safety is my top priority.
            Can you tell me more about what's been happening?"

Student: "I've been cutting. When I feel overwhelmed, 
          it's the only thing that helps."

Counselor: "I hear you. It sounds like you've been going through 
            something really difficult, and you found a way 
            to cope with those overwhelming feelings. 
            
            I want you to know that you're not alone in this. 
            Many young people experience similar feelings. 
            
            First, I want to make sure you're safe right now. 
            Are you in any immediate danger?"

Student: "No, I'm not going to do anything drastic."

Counselor: "I'm glad to hear that. 
            I'd like to work with you on a safety plan — 
            a list of things you can do when you feel overwhelmed 
            instead of hurting yourself. 
            
            I also want to connect you with additional support. 
            With your permission, I'd like to involve 
            our school psychologist and your parents. 
            
            How does that sound?"

Student: "I'm scared to tell my parents."

Counselor: "I understand. That's completely normal. 
            We can talk about how to approach that conversation. 
            I can be there with you if you'd like. 
            
            Remember, telling your parents isn't about getting in trouble — 
            it's about getting you the support you deserve. 
            
            Let's start with the safety plan, and then 
            we'll figure out the next steps together."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

#### Scenario 2: Parent Conference — Behavioral Concerns

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
📍 Phòng họp trường
👥 NHÂN VẬT: Parent + Counselor (bạn) + Teacher
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Counselor: "Thank you for coming in today, Mr. and Mrs. Nguyen. 
            I appreciate you taking the time. 
            
            I want to start by saying that we truly care about 
            Minh's success and well-being. 
            
            We've noticed some changes recently that we'd like 
            to share with you. Ms. Tran, would you like to start?"

Teacher: "Minh has been less engaged in class lately. 
          Their grades have dropped, and they seem withdrawn."

Counselor: "Thank you, Ms. Tran. 
            Mr. and Mrs. Nguyen, I'd like to hear your perspective. 
            Have you noticed anything at home?"

Parent: "Minh has been spending more time alone in their room. 
         They used to talk to us, but now they're very quiet."

Counselor: "Thank you for sharing that. 
            It sounds like we're all seeing similar patterns. 
            
            I want to emphasize that this doesn't mean 
            something is 'wrong' with Minh. 
            Adolescence is a time of significant change, 
            and many students go through difficult periods. 
            
            What I'd like to suggest is a collaborative approach. 
            At school, we can provide counseling support 
            and check in with Minh regularly. 
            
            At home, you might try:
            - Creating a regular time to talk without distractions
            - Showing interest in their activities
            - Being patient and non-judgmental
            
            Let's also develop a behavior plan together. 
            
            Would you be open to meeting again in two weeks 
            to see how things are progressing?"

Parent: "Yes, we want to help however we can."

Counselor: "That's wonderful. Remember, you're not alone in this. 
            We're partners in Minh's success. 
            Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have concerns 
            before our next meeting."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 💡 TIPS cho Tuần 10

```
🌟 Mẹo 1: HỎI TRỰC TIẾP VỀ SELF-HARM/SUICIDE
   → ❌ Tránh né chủ đề = nguy hiểm hơn
   → ✅ "Are you thinking about hurting yourself?"
   → ✅ "Have you thought about suicide?"
   → Hỏi trực tiếp KHÔNG làm tăng nguy cơ

🌟 Mẹo 2: LUÔN CÓ SAFETY PLAN
   → Warning signs → Coping strategies → Support contacts → Emergency numbers
   → Viết ra giấy cho học sinh giữ
   → Review và update regularly

🌟 Mẹo 3: VỚI PHỤ HUYNH — ĐỒNG MINH, KHÔNG ĐỐI ĐẦU
   → ❌ "Your child has a problem"
   → ✅ "We've noticed some changes. I'd like to hear your perspective"
   → Phụ huynh = đồng minh, không phải nguyên nhân

🌟 Mẹo 4: BIẾT GIỚI HẠN CỦA BẢN THÂN
   → Bạn là school counselor, không phải therapist
   → Nếu case phức tạp → referral
   → "I'd like to connect you with a specialist who can help"

🌟 Mẹo 5: CHĂM SÓC BẢN THÂN SAU SESSION NHẠY CẢM
   → Decompress: đi bộ, hít thở, nói chuyện với colleague
   → Debrief với supervisor
   → Bạn không thể giúp người khác nếu bạn kiệt sức
```
