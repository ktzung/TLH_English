### 🗣️ B. SPEAKING PATTERNS

#### Pattern 1: Opening a Class

```
"Good morning/afternoon, everyone. Welcome to [COURSE NAME]."
"Before we begin, how is everyone doing today?"
"Today we'll be covering [TOPIC]."
"By the end of this class, you'll be able to [LEARNING OUTCOME]."
"Let's start with a quick question: [QUESTION]?"
```

#### Pattern 2: Explaining a Concept

```
STEP 1: State the concept
"Today we're going to learn about [CONCEPT]."

STEP 2: Define it
"[CONCEPT] is defined as [DEFINITION]."

STEP 3: Give an example
"For example, [REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE]."

STEP 4: Use analogy
"Think of it like [ANALOGY]."

STEP 5: Check understanding
"Can anyone give me another example?"
"Does that make sense so far?"
```

**Ví dụ — Explaining "Scaffolding":**
> "Today we're going to learn about **scaffolding** in education.
> 
> Scaffolding is a teaching method where teachers provide **temporary support** to help students achieve a learning goal they couldn't reach on their own.
> 
> For example, when teaching essay writing, a teacher might first provide a **template**, then an **outline**, then gradually remove support until the student can write independently.
> 
> Think of it like **training wheels on a bicycle**. You need them at first, but eventually, you ride on your own.
> 
> Can anyone think of a time when they used scaffolding in their teaching?"

#### Pattern 3: Giving Instructions

```
"Here's what I'd like you to do."
"First, [STEP 1]. Then, [STEP 2]."
"You'll have [TIME] to complete this."
"When you're finished, [NEXT ACTION]."
"If you have questions, [WHAT TO DO]."
```

#### Pattern 4: Checking Understanding

```
"Does that make sense?"
"Is everyone with me?"
"Can someone summarize what we just discussed?"
"Before we move on, are there any questions?"
"Let me check for understanding. Can someone give me an example of...?"
"I want to make sure everyone understands. Who can explain...?"
```

### 🎤 D. SHADOWING EXERCISE

**Đoạn 1: Opening (25 giây)**
> "Good morning, class! Welcome to today's session on educational psychology. I hope you're all doing well. Today, we're going to explore a very important concept — motivation. By the end of this class, you'll understand the difference between intrinsic and extrinsic motivation."

**Đoạn 2: Explaining (35 giây)**
> "Let me explain the concept of intrinsic motivation. Intrinsic motivation refers to the internal drive to do something because it's personally meaningful or enjoyable. For example, a student who reads a book because they're genuinely curious — not because they'll get a grade — is intrinsically motivated. Think of it like the difference between eating because you're hungry versus eating because someone told you to."

**Đoạn 3: Closing (20 giây)**
> "Great work today, everyone. Let's review the key points. We learned that motivation can be intrinsic or extrinsic, and that intrinsic motivation tends to lead to deeper learning. For next class, please read chapter 5 and be ready to discuss."

### 🔤 E. PRONUNCIATION FOCUS — Classroom Voice

```
NÓI TO VÀ RÕ RÀNG:
"Good MORNING, EVery-one." → Nhấn "morning" và "everyone"
"Let's get STAR-ted." → Nhấn "started", giọng năng lượng
"Please turn to PAGE forty-five." → Nhấn "page"
"Any QUES-tions?" → Giọng LÊN cuối câu
"Well DONE!" → Nhấn "done", giọng khen ngợi
"Does that make SENSE?" → Giọng LÊN nhẹ cuối câu

TỐC ĐỘ:
→ Instructions: CHẬM và RÕ
→ Explanation: TRUNG BÌNH
→ Encouragement: NHANH và NĂNG LƯỢNG
→ Checking: CHẬM, chờ đợi
```

                 Constructivism is the idea that learners actively 
                 construct their own understanding of the world 
                 through experience. 
                 
                 Think of it like building a house — you add brick by brick, 
                 each representing new knowledge. 
                 
                 For example, when a child learns about gravity, 
                 they don't just memorize a definition. 
                 They experience it when they drop a ball and watch it fall. 
                 
                 Now, here's a question for you: 
                 How might constructivism change the way we teach?

                 [Wait 2 minutes]
                 
                 Okay, time's up! Who'd like to share? 
                 
                 [Hear responses]
                 
                 Excellent observations! 
                 
                 Let me summarize: Constructivism suggests that 
                 teachers should be facilitators, not just lecturers. 
                 We should create experiences that help students 
                 build their own understanding. 
                 
                 For next class, please read about Vygotsky's Zone

### 💡 TIPS cho Tuần 7

```
🌟 Mẹo 1: NÓI VỚI LỚP, KHÔNG NÓI VỚI BÀN
   → Ngẩng đầu. Nhìn "sinh viên" (camera)
   → Di chuyển nhẹ. Không đứng yên
   → Sử dụng tay để minh họa

🌟 Mẹo 2: PAUSE SAU CÂU HỎI
   → Hỏi xong → ĐỢI 5 giây
   → Sinh viên cần thời gian suy nghĩ
   → Im lặng ≠ lớp chán. Im lặng = đang suy nghĩ

🌟 Mẹo 3: SỬ DỤNG "THINK-PAIR-SHARE"
   → "Think" (1 phút): Suy nghĩ cá nhân
   → "Pair" (2 phút): Thảo luận cặp
   → "Share" (3 phút): Chia sẻ cả lớp
   → Giảm áp lực nói trước lớp

🌟 Mẹo 4: KHEN CỤ THỂ
   → ❌ "Good job" (chung chung)
   → ✅ "I like how you connected theory to practice"
   → ✅ "That's a creative example"
   → Khen cụ thể = động viên hiệu quả hơn

🌟 Mẹo 5: CHUẨN BỊ "BACKUP ACTIVITY"
   → Nếu hết bài sớm → thảo luận nhóm
   → Nếu sinh viên không hiểu → ví dụ khác
   → Luôn có kế hoạch B
```
